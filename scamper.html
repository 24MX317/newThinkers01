S SUBSTITUTE

üîÑ Transform Traditional Education

  • Fixed Classrooms ‚Üí Flexible Learning Spaces: Multi-purpose environments that adapt to different learning styles
  • Static Curriculum ‚Üí Industry-Responsive Modules: Dynamically updated content based on job market demands
  • Age-Based Classes ‚Üí Skill-Level Groupings: Competency-based learning communities
  • Teacher-Centric ‚Üí Mentor-Facilitator Model: Industry experts guiding practical learning
  • Textbook Learning ‚Üí Project-Based Practice: Real-world applications over theoretical study
  • Individual Assessment ‚Üí Peer & Self-Assessment: Collaborative evaluation methods

C COMBINE

ü§ù Powerful Combinations

  • Academic + Vocational Training: Theoretical knowledge with hands-on technical skills
  • Local + Global Perspectives: Community focus with international industry standards
  • Multiple Learning Styles: Visual, auditory, kinesthetic, and digital approaches
  • Intergenerational Learning: Students + working professionals + seniors together
  • Public-Private Partnerships: Government support with industry collaboration
  • Online + Offline Learning: Digital platforms with physical learning spaces

A ADAPT

üí° Learn from Success Stories

  • From Coding Bootcamps: Intensive, outcome-focused training models for various skills
  • From Community Centers: Open-access, community-driven programming approaches
  • From Corporate Training: Practical, job-ready skill development methodologies
  • From MOOCs: Scalable online learning with local support systems
  • From Maker Spaces: Collaborative, resource-sharing learning environments
  • From Microfinance: Income-share agreements or flexible payment models

M MODIFY

üìà Enhance & Optimize

  • Magnify Community Engagement: Expand beyond students to serve entire families
  • Minimize Infrastructure Costs: Use shared spaces, mobile units, existing facilities
  • Magnify Industry Connections: Amplify partnerships for internships and job placements
  • Modify Learning Duration: Flexible timeframes - weekends, evenings, intensive workshops
  • Magnify Accessibility Features: Enhanced support for disabilities, language barriers, economic constraints
  • Minimize Administrative Overhead: Streamline through technology and volunteer support

P PUT TO OTHER USES

üîÑ Maximize Resource Value

  • Learning Spaces as Community Hubs: Host meetings, events, social services during off-hours
  • Students as Teachers: Advanced students mentor beginners in peer-to-peer networks
  • Skills Lab as Service Center: Students practice by providing community services (IT, design)
  • Alumni Network as Recruitment: Graduates become ambassadors and talent scouts
  • Equipment for Community Access: Share learning tools and resources with broader community
  • Curriculum as Open Source: License teaching materials to other educational initiatives

E ELIMINATE

‚ùå Remove Barriers to Learning

  • Eliminate Entrance Barriers: No entrance exams, prerequisites, or discriminatory admission
  • Eliminate Fixed Schedules: Remove rigid timetables that exclude working students
  • Eliminate Expensive Textbooks: Use open-source and practical resources instead
  • Eliminate Hierarchical Structures: Remove traditional power dynamics for collaboration
  • Eliminate Irrelevant Curriculum: Remove outdated content not serving job market needs
  • Eliminate Geographic Barriers: Mobile learning and online options for all locations

R REVERSE

üîÑ Flip the Script

  • Reverse Payment Model: Employers pay for trained graduates they hire, not students paying upfront
  • Rearrange Learning Flow: Start with practical application, then teach underlying theory
  • Reverse Teaching Direction: Students teach community members, reinforcing their own learning
  • Rearrange Assessment: Real-world problem-solving rather than theoretical tests
  • Reverse Institution Structure: Student-governed with rotating leadership roles
  • Rearrange Funding: Revenue through student-run social enterprises, not tuition
